About the Crop:

Swarna (MTU 7029) is a versatile, high-yielding rice variety well-suited to the rainfed shallow lowlands of Bihar and other similar regions. It thrives in soils with moderate fertility, particularly clay loam to loamy soils.

The variety requires a medium level of water, generally needing 700-900 mm of water during the growing season. Swarna typically yields 22-24 quintals per acre under optimal conditions, offering good profitability due to its adaptability and lower input costs.

Farmers can expect decent profit margins, especially when grown in suitable soil and with efficient water management

  • Soil Composition: Loamy and Clayey Soil with Ph 5.5 to 7.5
  • Water need per Acre: 4 to 5 Feet per acre
  • Potential Dangers: Stem Borers, Sheath Blight, Rice Weevil, Salinity
  • Fertilizers: Urea, Ammonium Sulfate, Potassium, Zinc Sulfate
  • Pesticides: Chloropyrifos, Carbendazim, Butachlor
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹20,000 to ₹55,000
